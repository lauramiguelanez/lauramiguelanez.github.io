Laura Migueláñez is an architect, programmer and communication designer graduated from the ETSAM-UPM, educated at G30 Master at the University of Tokyo and B.phil. student at the UNED.

She currently is a researcher at the Architecture, Design, fashion and Society program UPM and communication manager at the Madrid School of Architecture (ETSAM). Her practice encompass the disciplines of architecture, digital morphology, communication and web development, exploring the friction between domesticity and digital technologies in the radical present.

Within the architecture field she has work since 2009 in many architecture studios such as AMID.cero9, C+ Architects or Izaskun Chinchilla Architects and she received the Arquia Foundation best business venture idea Grant 2017. She has contributed to projects ranging from exhibition, ephemeral architecture, publications, building and computational construction.

She’s been editor in chief (2014-15) of the experimental editorial platform 255 daetsam. Since 2016 she is part of the teaching team of the master’s program MIAU (ETSAM), a pedagogical approach to multimedia design from the deep understanding of processes, system generation and data managing through algorithms, simulations and digital ecosystems, based on technical, critical and creative mastery.
